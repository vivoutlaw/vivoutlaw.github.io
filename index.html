I am a research assistant and a 2nd year PhD candidate at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology. I work at the Department of Informatics - Institute for Anthropomatics and Robotics with the Computer Vision Group, CV:HCI. I am advised by Prof. Rainer Stiefelhagen. Earlier, I worked as a research assistant at KU Leuven with the Computer Vision Group where I was advised by Prof. Luc Van Gool (KU Leuven/ ETH Zürich).

I completed my M.Sc. CIMET joint-degree in computer science with majors in computer vision and multi/hyper-spectral imaging at UJM, UGR, NTNU, and KIT. I completed my B.Tech. in computer science at B.K. Birla Institute of Engineering & Technology, Pilani. Prior to my enrolment into the Masters, I have worked at Fraunhofer IOSB and KIT as a research associate and a guest researcher.

My recent research work has been related to topics computer vision, machine learning, multi/hyper-spectral imaging, image processing, and safe human-robot collaboration.

Past european funded projects that I have contributed to are: CADaVISION, ViMuDat, AMIKA, ROVINA. Presently working on PLUMCOT.

I always seek new collaboration. If you are interested in working with me, I will be glad to hear from you.

If you need to contact me, the best way to reach me is via email or by skype.
